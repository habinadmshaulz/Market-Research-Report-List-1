<p><p>The PP nonwoven fabric market comprises several key segments: spunbonded, staples, meltblown, and composite. Spunbonded fabrics are produced through continuous filaments and are known for their strength and durability, often used in hygiene products. Staple fabrics utilize short fibers, offering a soft texture suitable for applications like wipes and apparel. Meltblown fabrics are created by extruding molten polymers, resulting in fine fibers ideal for filters and protective clothing. Composite fabrics combine different types for enhanced performance across various applications.</p></p>
